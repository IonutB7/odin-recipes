Pizza

Description

Pizza Capricciosa is a popular Italian pizza that is known for its toppings of ham, mushrooms, artichokes, and olives. It is said to have originated in Rome and is known for its unique combination of flavors and textures. The toppings are carefully selected to provide a balance of salty and savory flavors that complement the tomato sauce and melted mozzarella cheese

Pizza Capricciosa is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for lunch, dinner, or as a snack. It's typically made with a thin and crispy crust, but can also be made with a thicker and chewy crust for those who prefer a heartier pizza. It's a great option for anyone who enjoys a balanced and flavorful pizza that is packed with toppings. Whether you're a fan of classic Italian cuisine or simply love a good pizza, Pizza Capricciosa is sure to satisfy your cravings. Serve it hot and fresh from the oven for the best experience.

Ingredients

  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Ham
  • Mushrooms
  • Artichokes
  • Olives
  • Oregano
  1. Prepare the dough: Mix flour, yeast, salt, sugar, and water to form a dough. Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise for about an hour.
  2. Make the tomato sauce: Blend canned tomatoes, garlic, salt, and olive oil to make a smooth sauce.
  3. Preheat the oven: Preheat the oven to 220-240°C (450-475°F).
  4. Assemble the pizza: Roll out the dough to form a thin round crust. Spread a layer of tomato sauce over the crust, leaving a small border around the edges. Add sliced mozzarella cheese, ham, mushrooms, artichokes, and olives on top. Sprinkle with oregano.
  5. Bake the pizza: Place the pizza on a baking sheet or pizza stone and bake for 12-15 minutes or until the crust is crispy and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Slice and serve hot. Enjoy!
